How much a website costs for a small business in 2026

July 11, 2026·7 min read

The short answer: from zero to infinity. The useful answer: for a small business in Europe a working website costs €500 to €3000, and below we break down what makes up that price and where you can save without harm.

Three ways to get a website

DIY website builder: €0-300 per year

Wix, Squarespace, Tilda. You pay a subscription and build it yourself. Fine if you have time, taste and a simple task: a business card site with contacts and a few photos.

The catch: builder sites are easy to recognise, they load slower, and after a year of subscription you will have paid as much as a simple turnkey site costs. Most importantly: a business owner's time costs more than it seems.

Freelancer: €300-1500

Cheaper than an agency, quality unpredictable. A good freelancer will do a great job, but you still need to find them, vet them and accept the work. A common story: the site is delivered, the freelancer vanishes, and there is nobody to change the phone number on the page.

Agency or studio: €500 to €5000+

More expensive, but you are buying a result, not pages: a sales-oriented structure, copy, analytics and a person responsible for the site after launch.

What makes up the price

  • Structure and copy. The most underrated part. A beautiful site with weak copy does not sell.
  • Design and build. A template is cheaper, custom design costs more.
  • Number of pages. A one-page landing is cheaper than a 10-page site.
  • Technical setup. Domain, hosting, email, analytics, forms. Small things separately, half a day of specialist work together.
  • Support. Who makes changes after launch and what it costs.
Tip: don't ask "how much is a website", ask "what is included in the price". Two €800 proposals can differ several times over in what you get.

Hidden costs people forget

Domain (€10-30 per year), hosting (€0-100 per year), email (€0-6 per month per mailbox), SSL renewal (usually free, but check). And advertising: a website without traffic brings no customers, however good it is.

When a website pays off

Simple maths. If your average service ticket is €50 and the site brings 4 clients a month, that is €200 a month and €2400 a year. An €800 site pays for itself in 4 months. So the question is not "cheap or expensive" but "will it bring customers or not".
